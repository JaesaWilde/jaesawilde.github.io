Any% speedrun revolves around two major aspects:

  • Main objective completion
  • Death optimization

Since Any% and Extract% are distinct categories, extraction shall not be part of an Any% speedrun. On the other hand, mission must end in order for the run to be valid. The only way to do so without extracting is to burn reinforcements down to 0. Killing yourself with no reinforcements left will trigger the end of the mission.

A proper kill

To begin with, one needs a proper way to perform the most immediate death. So far, three weapons and one throwable have been identified:

Weapon/Throwable Warbond Page Cost
G-16 Impact Helldivers Mobilize 5th 20 Medals
R-36 Eruptor Democratic Detonation 2nd 60 Medals
CB-9 Exploding Crossbow Democratic Detonation 3rd 80 Medals
GP-31 Ultimatum Servants of Freedom 2nd 40 Medals

Amid these possibilities, the Explosive Crossbow is considered to be the best choice:

  • Faster animation than Impact Grenades
  • Able to one-shot heavy armored Divers while Eruptor cannot
  • the velocity of its ammunition is faster than the Ultimatum's

From a larger perspective, the Explosive Crossbow can close fabricators and bug nests. It also allows you to deal with light and medium enemies.

A "Run and burn" mission allows you to burn part of the reinforcements while main objective is being completed. However, you will likely get it done before having all the reinforcements burnt.

It involves then a precise strategy to burn as many reinforcements as you can without slowing down main objective completion. Left reinforcements will be burned in rounds as every diver will kill themselves as they get out of their pod.

These missions are the shortest ones. The strike force of your hellpod is enough to complete main objective. From its beginning to its end, the mission therefore consists in correctly guided hellpods and 6 rounds of frame perfect deaths.

Numbers don't matter

Every Helldiver comes to the front with 5 reinforcements:

Squad size Reinforcements
1 5
2 10
3 15
4 20

It means that no matter the size of the squad, you will need to die the exact same amount of time to trigger the end of a mission. Quitting during a run won't change this statement.

Assuming each Helldiver burns each of they five reinforcements, they will each have to die 6 times.

The number of reinforcements is always a multiple of the number of the Helldivers. Although not sticking to the multiple won't cause an additional round of death, it is advisable to all die at once. The whole squad will then respawn without loosing time to type and throw the reinforcement stratagem.

How to perform a frame-perfect death

Getting a top time on Run and burn & Fullpod missions obviously depends on how fast main objective can be completed but also on how swiftly post-completion deaths are performed.

To do so, you need to get used to executing the movement that allows you to die before you have even fully exited the pod.

Just after the pod lid pops off and your Helldivers's body stats to emerge from the pod, you need to engage a downward mouse movement to make your Helldiver look at the ground. Just as the Helldiver's thighs start to become visible, press the fire button without aiming.

To prevent the ammunition from glitching through textures as much as possible, land on the flattest, clearest surfaces possible. Make sure you release your moving keys as soon as your pod touches the ground. This will prevent you from ending up in a kind of crouching position - which is more likely to cause ammunition glitches if your shoot timing is the same as when your Helldiver exits the pod normally (standing up straight and pointing their weapon skywards).

Also, ensure you are as far apart as possible to avoid the explosive energy of your ammunition/grenade ragdolling the others.
