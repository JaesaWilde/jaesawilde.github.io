In the course of Fullpod or One and out runs such as Blitz: Search and Destroy or most of Eliminate missions, achieving top times requires to manipulate the respawn position of your hellpod. This will allow you either to move from one place to another (oupost/target location) in order to complete the objective, or to land on higher rocks and save enough milliseconds to get the lowest time possible.

Although every respawn position involves a fair degree of RNG - notably due to terrain composition and obstacles likely to podlock you (rock formations, buildings, trees, canyons, etc.) it is possible to manipulate this RNG by combining the three following elements: hellpod choice, camera orientation, torso warp.

Hellpod choice

Before choosing the landing point, you may pick one of four hellpods:

The position of the hellpod you choose will affect the position on the screen where you are more likely to respawn. In a multiplayer run, it is therefore advisable to choose your hellpod in accordance with the cardinal direction assigned to you. In a solo un-scouted run, we advise you to choose one of the middle hellpods for greater adaptability or the hellpod best suited to the map layout.

Camera orientation

No matter when or how you die, you still control your camera and are able to look around until the camera starts its upward movement to reach your reinforcement hellpod. During these few seconds, it is absolutely crucial to point your camera in the direction where you want you repod.

Torso warp

We have just mentioned the few seconds where you can control the orientation of your camera between the moment you die and the moment you respawn. The thing is during that moment, the camera remains "fixed" to your dead torso. Its last known position also counts in calculating where you are going to repod.

This is all the more visible when you kill yourself with the Eruptor. You may use your weapon to propel yourself in other to traverse greater distances without actually moving. As every Helldiver is right-handed, your dead torso will likely float to the left-bottom corner of your screen. This warp mechanic is of primary use for solo runs - especially Fullpod or One and out.
