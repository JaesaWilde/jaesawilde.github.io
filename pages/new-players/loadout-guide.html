  • Automatons spawners are called fabricators. Regular ones look like rectangular cubes while bulk fabricators (found in city maps) look like upright coffins.
  • Terminids spawners are called holes. Giant ones which spawn Bile Titans are called Titan holes.
  • Illuminate spawners are called warp ships.

If you're new to the game, don't have a ton of money to put into Premium warbonds and don't know what stuff to unlock first in order to be effective at low level, this page gathers recommendations from speedrunners' perspective and experience.

Gameplay style

Whether you play with friends or randoms, two common ways to play the game is either to group up and clear objectives together or split up and cover alone larger map portions. This guide is in favour of solo effectiveness.

Even if you play within a squad, the gameplay a lot of us consider to be the most effective is to be lethal and fast. The lesser enemies you fight, the better. The aim is to be extremely mobile in order to clear the map while outrunning enemies. This is why we recommend the following order of priority for buying Premium warbonds:

  1. Democratic Detonation
  2. Servants of Freedom
  3. Steeled Veterans
  4. Viper Commandos
  5. Urban Legends
  6. Chemical Agents
  7. Polar Patriots

Armors

Helmet and cape don't matter. Just pick what you like.

General suggestions

Since being stealthy and fast are main priorities, Scout is the main passive you may use (Markers placed on the map will generate radar scans every 2.0s. Reduces range at which enemies can detect the wearer by 30%). Helldivers Mobilize warbond contains three of them.

We recommend to equip the first one then the Trailblazer Scout one. It's of the lightest armors so you will tank very little damage but it is by far the best armor to outrun enemies since its stamina stats offset the lack of protection. It also allows you to perform greater leaps with Jump Pack.

Secondary weapons

One of the main reasons to buy Servants of Freedom Premium warbond is the Ultimatum. Even after its nerf, it's the most powerful secondary weapon in the game. It can one-shot regular fabricators, holes, shielded warp ships, Spore Spewers two-shot bulk fabricators and Command Bunkers and deal with heavy enemies (Hulks, Tanks, Bunker Turrets, Heavy Turrets, Chargers, Bile Titans, Impalers, Fleshmobs, Harvesters).

The other secondary weapons we may recommend are found in two different warbonds. Senator deals much more damage (it can kill Hulks if shot in the eye) than Verdict but it has less DPS and ammunition.

Throwables

Thermite grenades are a great choice against Automatons and Illuminate for they have a sticky effect and can destroy regular fabricators and warp ships from any direction. Idem for Mortar Emplacements, Anti Air Emplacement, Shreiker Nests and Spore Spewer. They can also deal with heavy enemies (Hulks, Tanks, Heavy Turrets, Bunker Turrets, Fleshmobs, Harvesters).

Another fine choice for both Terminids and Illuminate is Gas grenades, found in Chemical Agents warbond. They can close fabricators (if thrown in the vent), holes as well as un-shielded warp ships and they generate an area of effect that is long enough to either outrun enemies or clear a zone. Using Gas grenades along with Med-Kit armor may help you surviving tedious situations, especially against Terminids.

For a more specific use, Seeker grenades multiply kills (meaning a single enemy killed with a Seeker can count as 4). It is an oddity shared by Portable Hellbomb and Guard Dog K-9. You may therefore use Seeker grenades if you need to swiftly kill enemies in order to complete an objective:

  • Eradicate Automaton Forces / Eradicate Terminid Swarm
  • Purge Illuminate (side objective)

The fourth Booster slot is more flexible. You might want to bring the one matching your current need: more protection ( Vitality Enhancement), more stamina ( Stamina Enhancement) or faster extraction ( Expert Extraction Pilot).

We strongly recommend to chose Muscle Enhancement instead of Stamina Enhancement, for it helps you to get through a lot of environmental elements: acid storm (as well as acid inflicted by Terminids), sandstorm, blizzard, mud, snow, sand, uphills, shallow waters, bushes, etc. It is therefore way more useful in pretty much all planet biomes than Stamina Enhancement.

Stratagems

You only need to reach level 25 to unlock every useful stratagem. Beyond this threshold, levels only reflect your game time. While speedrunning sometimes requires very specific builds, an efficient casual gameplay (even at high level) revolves around common loadouts.

The one common denominator is Jump Pack since it allows you to traverse great distances, to access higher or lower areas of the map and outrun enemies. You may use it to infiltrate an outpost, take cover atop a flat rock during extraction, escape from deadly situations, leave a Fortress you just cleared from within, etc. No matter from where you leap, you won't take fall damage. We however recommend to release your moving keys not to get ragdolled as you land - especially if you were running just before leaping.

You should also be careful not to get too close to a downhill slope from which you are jumping, as fall damage counts from the moment the Jump Pack's thrusters ignite again (which happens as soon as the game detects ground close enough to your feet). But if the ground in question is a slope and you're only going to hit the bottom of it, you're likely to take damage.
